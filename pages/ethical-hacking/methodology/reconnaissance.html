Reconnaissance

Systematic information gathering that builds an accurate picture of the target environment before you send active probes or exploits.

What is recon in ethical hacking?

Reconnaissance is the process of learning as much as you can about a target using legal, authorized means. Good recon helps you understand where to focus later technical testing and how to minimize noise and risk.

In InfoSecKe labs, recon is modeled as a mix of open-source intelligence (OSINT) and target-specific information gathering that respects scope and rules of engagement.

Passive vs active reconnaissance

Passive recon is about observing what is already exposed without directly interacting with the target in a noticeable way. Active recon uses deliberate requests or connections, but still with low impact and within the allowed scope.

Typical recon activities in labs

In InfoSecKe scenarios, recon might involve gathering details from lab descriptions, fake company profiles, and intentionally exposed artifacts like changelogs or documentation pages.

Taking effective recon notes

Recon is only useful if you can refer back to it later. Many practitioners keep a running notebook per engagement, recording hosts, ports, technologies, credentials provided by the client, and any early hypotheses about where weaknesses might appear.

In labs, getting into the habit of structured note-taking will make your later exploitation and reporting much smoother.

Ethical boundaries during recon

Even at this early stage, it is possible to cross ethical or legal lines if you are careless. Staying inside scope matters: if a domain or application is not explicitly in scope for a lab or engagement, you treat it as out of bounds.

InfoSecKe is designed so that all recon is directed at environments created specifically for learning. You should carry that discipline with you into any real-world testing you do in the future.

Move from recon to scanning

Once you have a clear map of what appears to be in scope, you can move into scanning to confirm open ports, services, and technologies.
